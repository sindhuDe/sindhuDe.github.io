G20 CO2 Emissions vs GDP Growth Rate for 2019


Scatter plot showing the relationship between Average CO2 Emissions and Annual GDP Growth Rate for 2019.

Analyzing the correlation between GDP growth and CO2 emissions among G20 countries uncovers a fascinating relationship that merits close scrutiny.


A scatter plot illustrating this connection highlights the complex interaction between economic progress and environmental impact.


The increase in GDP growth rates among G20 countries is associated with a rise in CO2 emissions, as evidenced by the positively sloped scatter plot. This observation points to an interconnection between economic growth and carbon emissions, emphasizing the need for innovative strategies to reconcile sustainable development with environmental protection.


A closer examination of the scatter plot reveals that the correlation between GDP growth and CO2 emissions is significantly driven by countries with large carbon footprints, such as China and India.


As you proceed to the next slide, focus on the GDP growth rankings from different years since 2000 for the top five CO2 emitters among the G20 countries.
